Why to visit Kosova


Kosovo, officially the Republic of Kosovo, is a partially recognised state in Southeast Europe. It lies at the centre of the Balkans.According to Numbeo, which ranks destinations according to the cost of living, factoring in everything from pub drinks to bus fares, the cheapest country in Europe – and the sixth cheapest on the planet, behind only Pakistan, Sri Lanka, Libya, India and Algeria – is Kosovo.Kosovo is a land of remote, rugged mountains, home to ferociously independent people and stunningly untouched beauty. The Rugova Valley in the west, ringed by the spectacular peaks of the Accursed Mountains, stretches all the way to the Montenegrin border.

Historical places to visit in Kosova

Prizren Castle

Prizren Fortress (Albanian: Каlаја e Prizrenit) is a hilltop fortification in Prizren in Kosovo[a]. It overlooks the Prizren River which flows through Prizren, which developed around the fortress. The site of the fortress of Prizren has seen habitation and use since the Bronze Age (ca. 2000). In late antiquity it was part of the defensive fortification system in western D ardania and was reconstructed in the era of eastern Roman Emperor Justinian.

Jashari's Cemetery

The Memorial Complex Adem Jashari is a monument in Donji Prekaz, Skenderaj, Kosovo. It is categorized by the government as "Architectural" and was built in honor of the family and life of the Kosovo Liberation Army fighter Adem Jashari, killed in action during the Kosovo War on March 7, 1998, during what was later known as the Attack on Prekaz.
